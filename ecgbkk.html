Result : All Normal All good , no blockage on any artery , blood flow supply and heart wall blood perfusion all good under stress



PROBLEM with the PTTEP offshore medical in Bangkok, November 2022. ECG stress test was reported - ‘Equivocal’ - they can’t tell & Medical requested a heart scan.

Waiting time for the scan & cost did not add up so had to call it a day on the available DD job. (PDF scans, ECG and report available)



Kuala Lumpur : Stress Test Nuclear Myocardial Perfusion Scan , stress induced dipyridamole injection , 3.8- to 7-fold increase in CBF velocity. The scan confirms if there are arterial blockages and checks blood supply perfusion within the heart walls for heart damage


The ‘equivocal’ ECG indicated a ‘possible’ artery blockage (they can’t tell)

The Stress Test Nuclear Perfusion Scan PROVED NO blockages on any heart arteries

normal heart blood flow , normal heart wall blood perfusion under stress



Nuclear Perfusion Scan Result : As Report above - (hover to expand)

The left ventricular cavity size is normal
Images show normal myocardial thickening and normal wall motion
The calculated left ventricular ejection fraction is 69% at post stress and 61% at rest (normal)
Normal myocardial perfusion study
There is no stress induced ischemia seen in this study
The global left ventricular function is normal



Wellness Centre - Bangkok stress test ECG indicated a borderline ST depression. They may have disregarded that it was a ‘rapidly’ upsloping segment

Literature states the ST point should be increased for a rapidly upsloping segment

They reported Upsloping (however the ECG indicates rapid upsloping)

Horizontal or downsloping ST segment confirms a blockage
