Stuck Pipe Prevention


Stuck Pipe Prevention Supervisors Course : The Well Engineering School - Warri Nigeria




Stuck Pipe Prevention not on the DD training agenda of the two directional companies I worked for however I had the opportunity to do a DSV supervisors course

NPT and lost in hole charges can get staggeringly high & a huge risk for Small - Time operators


The first 3 years of my DD work was an apprenticeship in drilling a hole & a lot more than taking care of directional business



Thanks to

One year of land rigs with small time operators in Texas

Followed by 2 years of land rigs in Algeria with problem shales problem sands & insert bits

PDC bits too expensive for these operations & add in 3 years of watching insert bits like a hawk



Small time operators in Texas drill with water & put up with aggravated hole conditions

Tripping in hole was a stress - until it didn’t become stressful any more & if running insert bits there’s a lot of trips , hammering & hard reaming BHA’s back to bottom

On the drill floor all the time & your job was to keep these small time operators outta trouble



All came in useful when I moved to larger offshore operations in Nigeria

Nigerian operations were initially 45 deg tangent wells drilled without challenge & fast ROP’s all done in a week & next well

Then things CHANGED entirely when ALL development changed to horizontal wells



Shales became a big problem at high angle & all that Texas experience came in handy when trying to get BHA’s back to bottom with tight hole & heavy packing

On bottom over 67 deg inclination stick slip sliding , weight hanging & stalling with PDC bits & stuck in hole in a minute if you lost the now small 20 psi differential

Trips to run insert bits for landing & sliding in lateral sections & all the insert bit ‘know when’ knowledge came in right handy

(Problems solved by switching to RSS )



Onshore Thailand 1 year , motors & problem shales & insert bits & back to hands on work again – GREAT STUFF



Anyway

If a job calls for more than DD , I’m ok with that

Often land jobs in remote locations & the DSV needs all the help he can get to keep things running smooth
