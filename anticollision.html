The DD toolbox comes with high level application of anti-collision procedure

Gulf of Thailand , 4 years batch drilling , typically 16 wells on a 24 slot jacket

Production line - batch drilling



EVERY TOP HOLE 12 1/4" SECTION DRILLED IN 1 HOUR (max 1.5 hr)

& 3 HOURS LATER DRILLING THE NEXT ONE

Come 8 1/2" HOLE - DRILLING A NEW ONE EVERY DAY x 16



Under instruction to drill as fast as possible , make hole with control - anti collision critical same as any platform - BE ready to act fast

A complete anti-collision drilling plan HAS to be done in advance 2-3 days while they install the derrick from the drillship



Drilling tophole in 1 hour you have to know where it HAS to go & on hand over you make sure your relief knows where everything needs to go on the next one

The only leeway to improve well placement (& always try to improve a planned well placement) is high or low which the 8 ½” can take care of or tinker with KOP

You need to have your drilling plan in place because things are going to happen very fast



Running standard anti collision analysis on TOP HOLE plans & reviewing Clearance Factors is not good enough , the analysis on ‘plans’ always comes up fine

On Top Hole all wells are close - in advance check & review the centre-centre distances on wells (& planned wells) aside of you , particularly on wells which crossover these can be around 6 feet centre to centre , KNOW this & how close before you drill

SET parameters each well for your drill plan preferred high or low and no go zones - Have a drill plan ready to go for the optimum well placement

& Then you have to make it go there & things happen very fast

Projected Surveys to TD already in your anti collision panel and tinker the projections as you go



After batch drilling the top hole comes batch drilling of 8 ½” hole , drilled in 8 hours or 10 hours if its a pregnant lady (image 4 above).

Aiming for 550 ft/hr ROP's . Drilling a new 8 ½” hole every day is busy





Training : c/o Halliburton & Schlumberger Anti-Collision Training & Procedure

Practice : Know Protocol & Communication



SOME PERSONAL CHECK RULES NOT SEEN IN MOST PROTOCOLS ;

IN ADVANCE USE YOUR EYES & CONFIRM



The database is not always correct , Examples ;

New project start up , 9 months landwork in Thailand . Day 1 on location looking around & within 150 meters are un expected wellheads , call town for the surveys & update the database , 50% of locations had wells not on the database


Warri swamps , Drillship Parker 74 . Always check the new jacket after a rig move pays off ! ;

Normally , an accurate database , BUT one time after eye balling the new jacket THERE IS an unexpected existing well head - call town for the survey data . Worth checking the field maps too when sailing down the swamp rivers on a rig move - put a visual to the closest wellheads on the way to any new location



Offshore

Visual on the neighbouring platforms , make sure they are on the field database

As soon as on location - visit the platform jacket & check the slot map with what you see , check all existing wellheads are on database



After pre job anti collision analysis – one last thing

Ready to go , on the way to the rig floor make sure the rig has skidded to the correct slot
